Florio:UI & UX Research Case Study

Introducing Florio

all-in-one solution to provide easily discoverable plant care instructions and faster connections with local plant experts

Role: UX/UI Designer & Researcher Team Member
Duration: 3 Weeks
Tools: Miro, Figma, Zoom, AdobeXD
Project Type: Group Research & Design School Project, iOS Mobile App

The Problem

Background

Heading into this research project, we had a hypothesis many that many plant-loving Torontonians faced an issue. An issue accounting for the lack of space within the city, for plant-lovers to thrive. Our hypothesis began assuming Toronto's lack of space meant many plant-owners faceed issues with not having sufficient space to plant their gardens.

Problem Statement

After conducting survey and user interviews, we were able to conclude our issue we assumed many Torontonians faced, was false. In actuality, the data suggested people with plants have difficulty locating information on plant identification and instruction and are overwhelmed, lost, thus killing their plants.

How might we design a platform that can be an all in one source of information for guidance, caring, and advice from other peers to help any plant owner care for their plants?

User Insights

Listening to our Users

Initially, we aimed to connect plant owners for cutting and plant trading, but our users had other pressing needs. Our research unveiled an unmet demand for reliable and localized plant care guidance.

Interview Testimonies

"I don't know how much to waer my plants"

"I am facing challanges with basic watering. I can't water my plants properly."

So what is our path to a solution?

We looked and prioritized what our users were telling us.

1. Step by step care instructions

Catering knowledge to all plants, no matter how big or small. Florio will provide step by step caring instructions to minimize the frustrations users can face when dealing with their green babies.

2. A sense of community

Respecting the knowledge of the wise, plant enthusiests can now feel secure with plant are advice given from credible sources.

3. Care Specialist in proximity

Users can create and begin conversations with Florio's Green-Thumb Experts. Plant owners, garden ownerts or green thumbs, now have a platform of their own.

4. Calendar reminders

Never miss watering again. It was evident that many users felt the urgency in remembering plant tasks. So let us handle it for you.

The Results

In the development of the Florio app, user insights have played a pivotal role in shaping and refining its features. Through rigorous user research and feedback loops, we have actively sought to understand the unique needs, preferences, and pain points of our target audience.

  • Lo-Fi Iteration

    In the above sketch, we carefully organized the contents with a keen understanding of user behaviors and preferences obtained from our research. Each element was strategically placed to optimize user experience and enhance usability. By prioritizing and arranging the components thoughtfully, we aimed to streamline navigation and ensure intuitive interactions.

  • Mid-Fi Iterarion

    By transitioning from the lo-fi wireframe to a midtype prototype, we aimed to strike a balance between functionality and aesthetics, offering a more comprehensive representation of the final product. This stage serves as a crucial checkpoint for validating design assumptions, refining interactions, and ensuring that the evolving solution aligns with both user needs and business objectives.

  • Hi-Fi Iteration

    The transition to the high-fidelity prototype represents the culmination of an iterative design process, where careful consideration is given to every detail. The result is a visually stunning and highly functional prototype that aligns with the project goals and meets the needs and expectations of the end-users.
